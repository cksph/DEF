## I. Purpose & Scope

This report extracts verified CEO compensation benchmarks from recent SEC filings to support sensitivity testing of fair market compensation for a client CEO. The four companies — a restaurant brand (CAVA), a fintech neo-bank (Chime), a healthcare AI platform (Tempus AI), and a healthcare payments SaaS company (Waystar) — are recently-IPO'd, high-growth businesses with revenues in the range of approximately $650M–$1.1B (FY2024–2025), making them useful comparables for executive compensation positioning.

**All dollar figures are drawn directly from the SEC filings and verified against source documents.**

## IV. Key Sensitivity Variables for Client Compensation Analysis

### 1. Base Salary
| Scenario | Range | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Low (founder/co-founder) | $500K | Equity-heavy founder who accepts below-market cash |
| Below-market | $650K | Non-founder, early post-IPO, high equity component |
| Market midpoint | $725K–$750K | Growth-stage, scaling revenue, non-founder CEO |
| At-market / high | $800K–$840K | Established SaaS/healthtech CEO, meaningful profitability |

### 2. Annual Bonus Target (% of Base)
| Scenario | Target % | Max % |
|---|---|---|
| No plan | 0% | — |
| Standard | 100% | 150%–200% |
| Above-market | 110%–125% | 150%–200% |

### 3. Annual LTI Grant Value
| Scenario | Range | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| Contractual minimum | ~$3M | Time-vested RSUs |
| Mid-stage growth | $5M–$15M | RSUs + performance RSUs |
| High-growth / public | $25M–$40M | RSU/PSU split; 3–4 year vest |
| Transformative / founder | $40M–$100M+ | PSU-heavy; milestone-based |

*LTI typically represents 65%–97% of total direct compensation for high-growth tech CEOs.*

### 4. Severance Cash Multiple
| Scenario | Non-COC | COC |
|---|---|---|
| Lean / founder | 0× | 0× cash + equity acceleration |
| Conservative | 0.5× salary | 1× salary |
| Market standard | 1× salary | 1× (salary + target bonus) |
| Generous | 1.5× salary | 1.5× (salary + target bonus) |

### 5. Key Design Flags
- **Equity acceleration:** Always double-trigger; always 100% in this peer set
- **Gross-up:** No company provides 280G gross-up; using one would be an above-market outlier
- **Bonus plan eligibility:** Founder CEOs may be excluded; non-founders should be enrolled
- **Stock ownership guidelines:** 6× base salary for CEO is common (confirmed in Waystar — DEF 14A p. 47); 5-year transition period

## V. Anomalies & Red Flags

| Issue | Company | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0 CEO severance outside COC | Tempus AI | Lefkofsky receives no cash severance, no COBRA, no equity acceleration outside a COC. Founder-only arrangement; not portable | DEF 14A pp. 40–42 |
| Minimal non-COC cash ($250K) | Chime | At $500K base, Britt's 6-month severance = $250K — below market for a CEO of Chime's scale. Co-founder context explains it | DEF 14A p. 59 |
| Identical COC/non-COC cash | CAVA | Cash severance does not increase in a COC scenario. COC premium is delivered entirely through equity acceleration (~$78M) | DEF 14A pp. 37–39 |
| Salary earned ≠ rate | Waystar / Tempus | Waystar SCT shows $830K (blended; April step-up); Tempus shows $783K (mid-year start). Use $840K / $800K as current rates | DEF 14A p. 44 (WAY); p. 39 (TEM) |
| Waystar severance not extractable | Waystar | Termination benefits table uses vector/image rendering; cash multiple and dollar amounts not extractable from proxy PDF. Requires review of exhibit agreements in 10-K | DEF 14A pp. 53–55 |
